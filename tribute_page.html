Peter Ilyich Tchaikovsky

Romantic music composer

A portrait of Peter Ilyich Tchaikovsky and his signature.

Here's a timeline of Peter Ilyich Tchaikovsky's life:

  • 1840 - Born in Votkinsk, Russia.
  • 1845 - Tchaikovsky began piano lessons at age five. Precocious, within three years he had become as adept at reading sheet music as his teacher.
  • 1854 - Tchaikovsky lost his mother, and for the first time he seriously composed a waltz in memory of his mother.
  • 1859 - Tchaikovsky decided to give up his legal career and enter the St. Petersburg Conservatory to study composition. This was an important turning point in his life, and it was also the beginning of his own music creation. During his time at the conservatory, he was influenced by Western music theory and techniques, while also retaining the characteristics of Russian national music. His teacher Anton Rubinstein was of great help and encouragement to him, and also gave him a lot of criticism and suggestions.
  • 1865 - Tchaikovsky graduated with honors from the St. Petersburg Conservatory of Music. His graduation work was the cantata "Ode to Joy" (a poem by J.C.F. Schiller), which won a silver medal. In the same year, at the invitation of N.G. Rubinstein, Tchaikovsky came to Moscow, taught at the newly established Moscow Conservatory of Music, and began intense creative activities.
  • 1877 - Tchaikovsky married А.И. Milyukova.
  • 1876 - Tchaikovsky established a correspondence friendship with Mrs. Meck, which gave Tchaikovsky great spiritual comfort.
  • 1891 - Tchaikovsky went to the United States to conduct and perform his own music production.
  • 1893 - Tchaikovsky received an honorary doctorate from the University of Cambridge, England, and personally conducted the first performance of his Sixth Symphony in St. Petersburg on October 28. He died of cholera at the age of 53.
  • Musical Style - Tchaikovsky's music came in many types and styles, including operas, ballets, symphonies, concertos, fantasy overtures, etc. His music received international acclaim during his time and remain one of the representatives of Romantic music today.
  • Musical pieces - Some of Tchaikovsky's musical pieces are inspired by historical events or literary works, such as 1812 Overture, Romeo and Juliet, etc. some express his personal emotions and mood, such as Sixth Pathétique Symphony. some show the characteristics of Russian national music, such as Swan Lake, Nutcracker, etc.
